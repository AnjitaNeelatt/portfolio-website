    M.Tech: Remote Sensing & GIS [NITK]

    My doctoral research in remote sensing focuses on harnessing advanced geospatial techniques for tracking and monitoring desert locusts. By integrating doppler weather radar data, satellite imagery, spatiotemporal analysis, and machine learning models, I aim to enhance tracking and monitoring capabilities for desert locusts. Throughout my academic journey, I have explored diverse remote sensing applications, from coastal vulnerability assessment to shoreline dynamics detection. Additionally, I have worked extensively with digital image processing and machine learning, utilizing these technologies to address a wide range of geospatial challenges within the field.
